Framing the problem¶

This lab is focused on building a model that will predict the development of diabetes one year from baseline. We are going to create various models, support this, and calculate their performance through metrics such as R-squared, Mean Absolute Error, and Mean Absolute Percentage Error. The prediction will be used to identify patients who are at risk.

Insights of EDA¶

Since the correlation is positive and moderate with regard to disease progression, BMI is a good candidate for univariate regression analysis. The rest of the features, s5 and bp for example, are likely to have some association with the target and could be investigated in multivariate models. Disease progression is approximately normally distributed with a slight positive skew.

In [37]:
train_x_bmi = train_x[['bmi']]
val_x_bmi = val_X[['bmi']]
test_x_bmi = test_X[['bmi']]

scores_train = []
scores_val = []
mae_train = []
mae_val = []
mape_train = []  
mape_val = []   

degrees = [0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

for degree in degrees:
    poly = PolynomialFeatures(degree)
    X_train_poly = poly.fit_transform(train_x_bmi)
    X_val_poly = poly.transform(val_x_bmi)
    
    model = LinearRegression()
    model.fit(X_train_poly, train_y)
    
    pred_train_y = model.predict(X_train_poly)
    pred_val_y = model.predict(X_val_poly)
    
    r2_train = r2_score(train_y, pred_train_y)
    r2_val = r2_score(val_y, pred_val_y)
    value_mae_train = mean_absolute_error(train_y, pred_train_y)
    value_mae_val = mean_absolute_error(val_y, pred_val_y)
    value_mape_train = mean_absolute_percentage_error(train_y, pred_train_y)
    value_mape_val = mean_absolute_percentage_error(val_y, pred_val_y)
    
    scores_train.append(r2_train)
    scores_val.append(r2_val)
    mae_train.append(value_mae_train)
    mae_val.append(value_mae_val)
    mape_train.append(value_mape_train)
    mape_val.append(value_mape_val)
    
    print(f"Polynomial Degree {degree}:")
    print(f"Train R-squared: {r2_train}, Validation R-squared: {r2_val}")
    print(f"Train MAE: {value_mae_train}, Validation MAE: {value_mae_val}")
    print(f"Train MAPE: {value_mape_train}, Validation MAPE: {value_mape_val}")

results = pd.DataFrame({
    'Degree': degrees,
    'Train R2': scores_train,
    'Validation R2': scores_val,
    'Train MAE': mae_train,
    'Validation MAE': mae_val,
    'Train MAPE': mape_train,
    'Validation MAPE': mape_val
})
